My Time in the Field with GCCD

Over the past two summers, I worked with the Greene County Conservation District, gaining hands-on experience in environmental fieldwork and local conservation efforts. Through this internship, I was involved in data collection, site assessments, and learning how conservation practices are applied at the local level. This website highlights the work I contributed to, the skills I developed, and what I learned about the importance of protecting natural resources in my community.

Featured Experience

During my internship with the Greene County Conservation District, I helped set up gravid mosquito traps at various sites across the county. These traps are placed near standing water and are designed to attract mosquitoes that are ready to lay eggs, allowing for targeted sampling in the field.

After collection, the traps were processed and the mosquitoes were sent for testing to monitor for the presence of mosquito-borne diseases in the area. This work involved traveling to different locations, setting up equipment in the field, and assisting with environmental monitoring efforts across the county.

Why This Work Matters

The work I assisted with during my internship focused on field-based monitoring and assessment efforts that support environmental and public health goals in Greene County. This included setting up mosquito gravid traps and conducting tick drags, both of which help track the presence of disease-carrying insects in different areas of the county.

I also participated in field site visits with district staff to help evaluate stream conditions, ensure regulatory standards were being met, and assess potential sites for future stream restoration projects. These experiences showed how routine fieldwork contributes to larger decisions about land management, water quality, and conservation planning.
